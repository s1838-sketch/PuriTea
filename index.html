Why PFAS Matter

PFAS (Per- and Polyfluoroalkyl Substances) are “forever chemicals” that resist breakdown and accumulate in the environment and organisms. Exposure is linked to cancer, thyroid and liver dysfunction, and immune problems. Packaging, contaminated soil, water and certain coatings are common sources.

Problem Statement

Many teas claim “organic” yet may carry traces of harmful chemicals due to contaminated soil, pesticides, or PFAS-containing packaging. PuriTea’s mission: produce genuinely pure herbal tea — no PFAS, no plastic contact.

Inspiration

Inspired by investigative research (e.g., Veritasium’s report on PFAS contamination) — we focus on transparent sourcing and glass packaging to avoid contamination pathways.

Drying, Mixing & Packaging

  1. Harvest: Handpick fresh tulsi, hibiscus and other herbs.
  2. Wash & Dry: Gently rinse; shade or sun-dry for 2–3 days until crisp.
  3. Mix: Lightly crush leaves/petals — do not powder. Measure per blend ratios and combine.
  4. Store: Fill airtight glass jars; label name, ratio and date. No plastics or teabags that could hold PFAS.

Packaging

Reusable glass jars, jute tags and simple paper info cards — clearly marked “PFAS-free”.

Testing & Feedback

Planned sensory testing with peers and teachers covering aroma, flavor, color and perceived freshness. We’ll collect ratings (1–5) and adjust ratios. Example feedback: “Slightly more mint” → updated ratio notes.
